How to avoid illegal recruiters

Work with a legal, transparent process. Here’s what to look for.

If anyone asks for money before you have a job or a signed contract, be careful — legitimate recruiters do not charge you.

Red flags (avoid)

  • Anyone asking for placement fee or “processing fee” before you get a job
  • Vague or unclear contract terms
  • No written explanation of who does what (recruiter vs. employer vs. support org)
  • Pressure to pay or sign quickly

What we do (Fuji-Mayon)

  • No placement fee. Ever. We do not charge you for finding a job.
  • We work with a Japanese government–registered support organization.
  • Clear steps and roles: we explain everything before you sign.
  • Support after you arrive in Japan (housing, life, work).
